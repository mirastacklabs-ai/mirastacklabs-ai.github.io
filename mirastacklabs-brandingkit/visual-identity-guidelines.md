# MIRASTACK LABS Visual Identity Guidelines

## 1. Identity source

The current live website logo uses a three-slat forward-leaning geometric mark plus the MIRASTACK LABS wordmark. This kit preserves that geometry and color logic.

## 2. Logo system

### Primary logo

- `logos/mirastack-logo-horizontal-dark.svg`
- Use on dark surfaces (`#090C10`, `#0F1318`, `#131920`).

### Secondary logo

- `logos/mirastack-logo-horizontal-light.svg`
- Use on light surfaces (`#F0F2F5`, `#FFFFFF`).

### Tertiary logo

- `logos/mirastack-logo-stacked-dark.svg`
- Use for square placements, social profile images, and product cards.

### Mark-only logo

- `logos/mirastack-mark.svg`
- Use for favicon, watermark, and compact UI spots.

## 3. Mark meaning

The three slats represent layered platform sovereignty:

1. Foundation and control plane integrity
2. Operational intelligence and flow
3. Observability and explainability at enterprise scale

The forward lean indicates speed, directional confidence, and engineered progression.

## 4. Color roles

### Core brand colors

- Primary Teal: `#00C9B1` (modern infrastructure intelligence)
- Signal Orange: `#FF671F` (operator action and urgency)
- Neutral Steel: `#CED4DA` (trust, control, governance)

### Supporting colors

- Graphite Black: `#090C10`
- Dark Layer: `#0F1318`
- Card Layer: `#131920`
- Body Text Dark Surface: `#DDE3EA`
- Body Text Light Surface: `#090C10`

## 5. Typography

- Primary: JetBrains Mono
- Fallback: Courier New, monospace
- Character style: technical, precise, and engineered

### Sizing baseline

- Body: 16px / 1.7 line-height
- Navigation and labels: 12px to 13px uppercase with increased tracking
- Section headings: clamp-based responsive scale (already used in current CSS)

## 6. Clear space and minimum sizes

- Clear space around logos: at least 1x slat width from all sides
- Minimum digital width:
  - Horizontal logos: 140px
  - Stacked logo: 96px
  - Mark-only logo: 18px

## 7. Do not rules

- Do not rotate, skew, or round logo geometry
- Do not add gradients to the mark
- Do not recolor slats beyond approved palette tokens
- Do not add glows, strokes, or shadows to logos
- Do not place low-contrast logo versions on noisy backgrounds

## 8. UI art direction

- Build in modular layers and strict grids
- Prefer hard edges over rounded consumer UI patterns
- Use accent sparingly for status and decisions, not decorative flooding
- Keep motion purposeful: reveal, chain progression, and state shifts
